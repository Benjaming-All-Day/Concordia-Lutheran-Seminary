Feel called to serve?

Discerning whether pastoral ministry is right for you involves a balance of inner conviction, community affirmation, and practical readiness. We pray and hope that we can help guide you through that process.

How do I know Pastoral ministry is right for me?

The Holy Spirit may stir an inner desire to preach and shepherd believers, but such feelings alone can be unreliable. A clearer sign of God’s call to pastoral ministry is the confirmation of your gifts by other believers, as the Spirit often works through people and tangible means. This external affirmation should be paired with honest self-reflection—recognizing both your ability to serve and your need for training. Ultimately, a formal call from a congregation confirms that God has equipped and chosen you to serve through Word and Sacrament as Christ’s servant.

Paths of Calling

Pastoral Studies

While Concordia's primary focus is to prepare men for the pastoral ministry of Lutheran Church-Canada and beyond, we do offer an number of other programs and sessional workshops aimed at equipping followers of Christ to be leaders in their church community.

Pastoral Studies

Diaconal Studies

This program is designed to prepare men and women to serve as deacons in congregations of Lutheran Church-Canada. Deacons are individuals who work alongside pastors and support them in the work of caring for God’s people. While deacons do not preach or administer the sacraments, they serve in countless other ways, including teaching adults, children and youth, visitation, evangelism, and, where their talents allow, congregational music.

Diaconal Studies

Open Studies

Concordia also offers learning opportunities for students who do not wish to enter a degree program. "Open Studies" permits students to take credit courses that are not part of program leading to a degree or diploma.

Open Studies

Seminars & Workshops

Concordia also seminars and workshops to professional ministry staff and interested congregation members in Christian churches in a variety of topics presented by permanent and sessional faculty.
